Introduction

The Diabetes Health Indicators Dataset is a cleaned version of the 2015 CDC BRFSS survey, made publicly available on Kaggle (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/alexteboul/diabetes-health-indicators-dataset ). It contains >250k survey responses and 22 variables spanning demographics (Age, Sex, Education, Income), health behaviors (Smoker, PhysActivity, Fruits/Veggies intake, HvyAlcoholConsump), and chronic conditions (HighBP, HighChol, Stroke, HeartDiseaseorAttack). The outcome Diabetes_012 has three classes (0=no diabetes or only during pregnancy; 1=prediabetes; 2=diabetes).

Objective

This project aims to summarize key health indicators related to diabetes and explore how diabetes prevalence varies across demographic and behavioral factors. Specifically, we (i) provide descriptive summaries of indicators across diabetes status, (ii) visualize diabetes prevalence across age and BMI categories, and (iii) fit a simple logistic regression model to examine how age, BMI, physical activity, and high blood pressure relate to diabetes status.

Figure 1 Diabetes prevalence by Age group

The plot shows a clear age gradient in diabetes prevalence. Younger adults (under 45) have very low rates, generally below 5%. Prevalence begins to climb steadily starting around age 4549 and increases sharply in older age groups, reaching above 20% after age 65. This pattern suggests that diabetes risk rises consistently with age, which aligns with known epidemiologic patterns for type II diabetes.
